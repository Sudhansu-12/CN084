Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Butter or ghee for cooking

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, mashed potatoes, chopped onion, green chilies, grated ginger, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, chopped coriander leaves, and salt.
  2. Knead the mixture into a soft dough, adding water as needed. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  4. Take one dough ball and roll it out into a small circle.
  5. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the circle.
  6. Bring the edges of the dough together to cover the filling and seal it properly.
  7. Flatten the stuffed dough ball with your hands and roll it out gently into a paratha.
  8. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat.
  9. Place the rolled paratha onto the hot tawa and cook until brown spots appear on one side.
  10. Flip the paratha and spread some butter or ghee on the cooked side.
  11. Cook until both sides are golden brown and crispy, pressing lightly with a spatula.
  12. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls and filling.
  13. Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or any side dish of your choice.
