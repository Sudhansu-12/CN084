Ingredients

  • 1 cup dosa rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for cooking

Steps

  1. Wash dosa rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds together. Soak them in water for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking, drain the water and transfer the mixture to a blender. Add some water and grind to a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter. Add salt to the batter and mix well.
  3. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume and becomes slightly sour.
  4. After fermentation, stir the batter gently.
  5. Heat a non-stick dosa tawa or griddle over medium heat. Sprinkle a few drops of water on the tawa to check if it's hot enough (the water should sizzle and evaporate immediately).
  6. Grease the tawa lightly with oil.
  7. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  8. Drizzle some oil around the edges of the dosa and cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.
  9. Flip the dosa using a spatula and cook the other side for a minute or until it's cooked through.
  10. Remove the dosa from the tawa and serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any preferred side dish.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining batter to make more dosas.
  12. Enjoy your delicious homemade dosas!
