Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as carrots, peas, capsicum), boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup boiled cauliflower, mashed
  • 1/2 cup boiled beetroot, mashed (optional, for color)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • 8-10 pav (bread rolls)
  • Butter for toasting pav

Steps

  1. Heat butter in a pan or kadai.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add boiled and mashed potatoes, mixed vegetables, mashed cauliflower, and mashed beetroot (if using). Mix everything together.
  8. Add a little water if the mixture is too dry. Mash the vegetables using a potato masher or back of a spoon while cooking.
  9. Cook the bhaji on medium heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a thick consistency and all the flavors are well combined.
  10. Meanwhile, slice the pav horizontally without cutting all the way through.
  11. Heat a tawa or griddle and toast the pav with some butter until they are golden brown and crispy.
  12. Once the bhaji is ready and the pav is toasted, garnish the bhaji with fresh coriander leaves.
  13. Serve hot pav bhaji with toasted pav, lemon wedges, and chopped onions on the side.
  14. Enjoy your flavorful and spicy Pav Bhaji!
