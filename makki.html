Ingredients for Makki ki Roti

  • 2 cups maize flour (makki ka atta)
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee or butter for cooking

Ingredients for Sarso ka Saag

  • 2 bunches fresh mustard greens (sarso saag), washed and chopped
  • 1 bunch spinach leaves, washed and chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Fresh cream for garnishing (optional)

Steps for Makki ki Roti

  1. In a bowl, mix maize flour, salt, and enough water to form a smooth dough.
  2. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a round shape (roti) using your hands or a rolling pin. The roti should be thicker than regular wheat roti.
  3. Heat a griddle or tawa over medium heat. Place a rolled roti on the hot griddle.
  4. Cook the roti on both sides until golden brown spots appear, applying ghee or butter on both sides while cooking.
  5. Remove the cooked roti from the griddle and repeat the process with the remaining dough portions.
  6. Keep the cooked rotis warm in a covered container until serving.

Steps for Sarso ka Saag

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  6. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add chopped mustard greens and spinach. Mix well and cook until they wilt down and become tender.
  8. Add garam masala and cook for a few more minutes.
  9. Remove the saag from heat and let it cool slightly.
  10. Blend the saag into a smooth puree using a blender or immersion blender.
  11. Return the puree to the pan and simmer for a few minutes until it reaches the desired consistency.
  12. Optional: Garnish with fresh cream before serving.

Serve Makki ki Roti and Sarso ka Saag together and enjoy!
