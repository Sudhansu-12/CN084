Ingredients

  • 2 cups idli rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Steps

  1. Wash idli rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together. Soak them in water separately for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking, drain the water from urad dal and grind it to a smooth batter using a little water. The consistency should be fluffy and airy.
  3. Drain the water from the idli rice and grind it to a coarse paste. Mix the rice paste with the urad dal batter.
  4. Add salt to the batter and mix well. The consistency should be like pancake batter, neither too thick nor too runny.
  5. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume and becomes slightly sour.
  6. After fermentation, stir the batter gently.
  7. Grease the idli molds with oil.
  8. Pour the batter into the idli molds, filling them about 3/4 full.
  9. Steam the idlis in a steamer for about 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  10. Remove the idlis from the molds using a spoon or knife.
  11. Serve hot idlis with coconut chutney, sambar, or any preferred side dish.
  12. Enjoy your soft and fluffy homemade idlis!
